Brainstorm gets live presence. When you share a whiteboard, everyone on the board now sees each other's cursors and selection move in real time, across devices. Open a shared note and its header shows an avatar stack of who else is viewing it. Presence is display-only — it grants no access on its own — and rides the same end-to-end-encrypted sync channel as your data, so the relay only ever sees ciphertext.

The in-app browser gains a per-site trust switch (**Settings → Privacy → Trusted sites**): mark a login-gated site like x.com as trusted and it works normally, while every other site keeps Brainstorm's strict tracker- and third-party-cookie-blocking default. And the shell now flips light/dark in lockstep with your open apps instead of trailing a beat behind.

macOS builds are signed with a Developer ID and notarized by Apple, so they open
without a Gatekeeper warning. Windows is currently unsigned (you may see a
SmartScreen "unknown publisher" prompt — choose **More info → Run anyway**).
Existing installs update in-app from **Settings → Updates**.

It's still a beta: keep backups of anything important. Your vault is a plain
folder you fully control.
